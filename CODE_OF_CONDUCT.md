# 📜 Code of Conduct

## Contributor Covenant — Adapted for THE  The Robo-Tech Forum

---

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of the THE  The Robo-Tech Forum (RTF) website project pledge to make participation in our community a **harassment-free experience for everyone**, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community**.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Behaviour That Strengthens Our Community

- **Be respectful** — Treat everyone with dignity, even when you disagree
- **Be constructive** — Offer helpful feedback, not harsh criticism. "This could be improved by..." > "This is wrong"
- **Be inclusive** — Use welcoming and gender-neutral language
- **Be patient** — Many contributors are students learning for the first time. Help them grow.
- **Be collaborative** — Share knowledge freely. Explain your reasoning in code reviews.
- **Be accountable** — Own your mistakes. Apologise when you mess up. Learn from it.
- **Give credit** — Acknowledge others' work and ideas
- **Stay on topic** — Keep discussions relevant to the project

### ❌ Behaviour That Will Not Be Tolerated

- **Harassment** — Intimidation, stalking, unwanted contact, or following (online or offline)
- **Discrimination** — Derogatory comments based on personal characteristics listed in Our Pledge
- **Trolling** — Deliberate provocation, inflammatory comments, or sustained disruption
- **Personal attacks** — Insulting, demeaning, or belittling others
- **Sexual content** — Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention
- **Doxxing** — Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Spam** — Unsolicited promotions, mass low-quality contributions, or AI-generated PR farming
- **Bad faith** — Deliberately wasting maintainers' time, submitting intentionally broken code, or sabotaging the project
- **Gatekeeping** — Discouraging newcomers from contributing because they're "not experienced enough"

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to **all project spaces**, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, comments, code reviews)
- Any project-related communication channels (WhatsApp groups, Discord, email)
- Public spaces where someone is representing RTF (events, social media, talks)

It also applies when an individual is **officially representing** the project in public spaces — for example, using an official project email address or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Email the maintainers** at the contact provided in the repository (or DM a maintainer directly on GitHub)
2. Include as much detail as possible — what happened, when, where, and any relevant screenshots or links
3. All reports will be treated **confidentially**

You will receive an acknowledgement within **48 hours** and a resolution within **7 days**.

### Who Enforces This?

The RTF project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They have the right and responsibility to **remove, edit, or reject** comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this policy.

### Enforcement Actions

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when responding to violations:

#### 1. ⚠️ Warning

**For:** First-time minor violations (e.g., rude comment, unconstructive feedback)

**Action:** A private written warning explaining the violation and why the behaviour was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. 🚫 Temporary Restriction

**For:** Repeated minor violations or a single serious violation

**Action:** Temporary ban from interacting with the project (commenting, opening issues/PRs) for a specified period (7–30 days). Violating the restriction may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 3. 🔴 Permanent Ban

**For:** Sustained pattern of violations, harassment, or a single egregious violation (e.g., hate speech, threats, doxxing)

**Action:** Permanent removal from all project spaces. The individual will be blocked from the GitHub organization.

## Special Notes for Our Community

### We're a Student Community

Many of our contributors are college students — some making their very first open-source contribution. We ask experienced contributors to:

- Be **extra patient** with newcomers
- **Explain** rather than just point out mistakes
- **Encourage** rather than gatekeep
- Remember that you were once a beginner too

### Code Reviews Are Learning Opportunities

When reviewing someone's PR:

- ✅ "This works, but using our shared animation variants from `lib/animations.js` would be more consistent. Here's how..."
- ❌ "You clearly don't know what you're doing."

**Every code review should leave the contributor more knowledgeable than before.**

### Disagree Respectfully

Technical disagreements are normal and healthy. When they happen:

1. Focus on the **code and ideas**, not the person
2. Provide **evidence or reasoning** for your position
3. Be open to being wrong
4. If consensus can't be reached, defer to the maintainers' decision
